Radio Play Assignment #4

Even in these hard times, we can work together in teams to create Art.  You will need to collaborate on-line and follow through with your portion of the team project for eveyone to be successful.  These are the steps you’ll need to follow.  If any team has a great idea on how we can modify and improve the process, please let me know.

Your Goal - Create a complete 7 to 8 minute Radio Play with the resources available to you on-line.  

1)  Connect with your team members through Google Apps to discuss your ideas and create the script.  I’d suggest that you start by sharing your Silent Movie Scenarios.  Choose one to be the basis of your Radio Play.  

You will need to document your work on a Google Document that I have created for you.  This step is important so I can keep track of your work and give feedback.  It’s also how I will know who has and who has not been involved in the project since I will be able to see who has been contributing to the Google Document.   Remember to keep all conversations on task and school appropriate.

You will need to type out your conversations as you have them, kind of like a Message Board.  Use it for discussing the plot, character development, rehearsal goals and the division of responsibilities.  Since it’s a shared document, not everyone needs to be on-line at the same time.  You will also type your entire script and sound cue information in this Google Document. 

In dividing the class up into teams, I’ve put male and female, experienced and inexperienced in each group.  Honesty Moment: Not all of the students in class have been active about getting on-line and doing the assignments.  While I have to put everyone’s name on the groups list, I realize that there may be students who may not take part in this project.  If you don’t get any input from a member of your team, keep working.  It’s my job to deal with the consequences. You should be getting a Shared Document link from me.  You can also connect to your Google Document by clicking below.

2)  Once you and your team have decided on the script, you should rehearse on your own and what you can via FaceTime, Zoom, etc.  There will be delays in the sound, so don’t allow yourself to get off task and be frustrated.  By halfway through the project timeframe, you should be ready to record your parts.  

Your ChromeBook has an access to a program called Soundtrap.  This is an on-line, sound editing website program that you and your team can use.  You need to record your lines in character and upload them to the website.  If your play has more characters than actors, you’ll need to create multiple voices.  Please be sure they sound different!  I think it may be easier to record your individual speeches and then upload them separately. Honesty Moment:  I think, maybe not.  Your job now is to mix your lines in with your team members making it sound as if you were all in one place when it was recorded. If your SoundTrap app on the ChromeBook is not working properly, try going to the Google Play App Store and downloading a newer version.  You can also try a ChormeBook program called Beautiful Audio Editor if Soundtrap is just too fussy.

3)  After the dialogue is edited, you need to add sound cues.  These should be sound effects, opening music, transition music between some scenes and closing music.  There are many places on-line to get music and sound effects.  One of the better ones is Orange Free Sounds.  

_________________________________________________________________

Quality sound cues and good dialogue can make the difference in the production. Radio Plays rely solely on the sound to tell the story.  Think about how just using a sound behind the dialogue or in between lines can imply the location/atmosphere/emotion/ …  Conversation between characters is vital, but using Direct Address/Narrator can give the audience a wealth of information.  

The Performing Arts Class has done this type of project many times over the years, but this is the first in this fashion.  Please have patience with each other and with the technology. If you find a good way to achieve the project that I have not mentioned, please share it.  Our goal is to continue to create quality Art.
