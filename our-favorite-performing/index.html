Our Favorite Performing Arts Apps

We all know that there are billions of Apps out there for your phone and tablet.  It’s impossible to keep up with every one since new ones come out each day.  I’d like to create a list of Canal Winchester Performing Arts favorite Apps for Music, Theatre and Dance.

If you have some on your device already, what are they?  Why do you like them?  If you don’t, head over to your favorite App Store, Apple or Android, and see what you can find.  

The Apps need be for Music, Theatre or Dance.  (No, your favorite game does not qualify.)  We all know about GarageBand, iMovie, different metronome and tuning Apps.  Can you suggest an App that people don’t know about?    Here are some of our favorite Apps.

Music

Theory/Composition Apps

ChordBot – This App works very similarly to Band-In-A-Box.  You can create long chord progressions with both simple and complex chord structures.  It’s a great way to compose backing tracks.  I also love that the progression can be saved as a MIDI or WAV file to be used in other programs.

General Musicianship

Musiclock – If you want to practice soloing, this is a great option.  The App has 20 different pre-loaded diatonic backing tracks from Classical to Heavy Metal styles.  Each style has suggested scales to use for soloing and can be changed to any key.

InTune – This App is great to work on your sense of pitch.  It gives you two pitches and you need to identify which is higher.  Of course it gets harder as you go along.

Tone - This app has ear training. It has chord recognition so you can learn the quality of different chords. Interval training and pitch training.

Acapella - This app lets you film yourself singing or playing and then record other parts while the first part plays in your headphones. You can do a lot with this whether you wanna practice harmonies or do songs with you playing every instrument.

Perfect Pitch - This app shows you a nice way to warm up your voice. You can sing all kinds of songs and record yourself to listen back on. It also has a special training program, it has different levels so once your voice has learned to the level and the good quality sound of singing you can move on. 

Singers Studio - This app first starts you off with checking your vocal range. It can and will tell you if you are an alto, soprano, base, tenor, and more. This is a good start out so you can see how you sing to something else then what you think you are. It has special guided voice lessons to help you excel and learn more. Also it will tell you about your practice time to see how much you work on your voice.

Voxercise - This app is very beneficial. It helps you do your warmups and exercises anytime and anywhere you want. It also has exclusive video lessons to show you and help teach you. It can also help you learn riffs and have good runs. 

Guitar Apps

ToneBridge- An app that emulates the settings that famous guitarists used on recorded songs. It requires you to buy a device that will allow you to connect your guitar to your phone, like an IRig. Once all that’s done, hook it up to your amp of choice and rock out.

Loop2Learn- An app where you can take a youtube video and slow it down to whatever pace you want and also choose a starting and end point, and loop that phrase you have chosen. It’s great for learning new songs on any instrument.

ChordBank- This app allows me to learn new chords, tune my guitar, gives mini lessons by professionals through videos, and strumming patterns. That's all that I use the app for. 

Ultimate Guitar- This app also teaches chords but it has whole song charts with lyrics and the chords to go with them. This app also allows you to customize tabs and chord progressions.

Guitar Tuna - This app is very useful if you haven’t memorized the way you’re supposed to tune a guitar, ukulele, or a bass. i use this and enjoy it because it enables me to tune my guitar in less than 5 minutes.

FenderTone-Amplitube – These are Guitar amplifier emulations.  Run you Guitar through your phone/tablet before your amp and create the sound of a highly sophisticated amp stack - enough to annoy your neighbors.

Listening Apps

AMPme - This app is also one of my favorites because it amplified sound if you don’t have an amplifier with you at the moment and it sounds almost exactly the same. 

SoundCloud - Listen to your favorite artists, or more underground artists that you love. What’s great about SoundCloud is that it provides a free way to listen to music, but you can also post your own. In recent years, lots of stars have been coming from SoundCloud. It creates a pathway for anyone to get their music heard.

Spotify - I use this because i have created playlists with the songs i like on it and it is able to be played wherever. This is a free app that allows you to listen to your favorite songs whenever you are.

Musi - simple music streaming - I love this app because it allows me to download most music I’d like for free as well it doesn’t have many problems like other apps, only issue is the not being able to listen without internet service or phone data.

Amazon music - I also like this app because it is exactly like other popular streams like Spotify and Pandora except it has unlimited skips and it doesn’t require internet service so in my opinion it’s better than everything.

Shazam - I love this app for the main reason that I forget a lot of song names and this app listens to a lyric and comes up with the entire song it is very helpful to me.

Theatre

Shakespeare – This App includes his complete works and commentary.  It will also offer you a Shakespeare quote of the day.  Yes, I’m a geek.

Stagehand – With this App, I can create the look of thousands of gels and how they look on a given surface.  It’s been a big help in deciding which colors projected on stage can create what attitudes.

iRFR – This is a specialized App, but VERY handy.  I can control the lighting board in the auditorium from my phone.  That means I can turn lights on and off from anywhere in the room.  I don’t have to walk to the control board as I’m setting lights for a show.

Memorize lines by heart – Memy -  I like it because it allows you to memorize your lines without struggling . And allows you to go through a process to get to memorizing the words without help 

(How To) Pronounce - This app is really good because as actors sometimes we struggle with how to say the words correctly such as in Shakespeare so this app is pretty good with helping you figure out how to say the word correctly 

Wunderlist - Is a good app for actors, and people who want to be doing theatre things. It helps you set reminders to know when you should be practicing and turning in your work. You can record and turn in and share your stuff with friends, people who do it with you or your director.

Speak Easy Voice Recorder - This app will let you listen and run, and repeat your lines to help you remember them and get comfortable. It can record you so you can give yourself feedback and go back and see your progress throughout time.

Proprompter - This app allows you to have your script ready so you can see and practice whenever. It lets you record and it also helps you set reminders. You can put when you need to have all the parts remembered and it will show you your lines and everything to practice before the date is over. 

Dance 

Sway: Magic Dance - I like this app because If you're a terrible dancer this app is pretty cool because you can just make random dance moves and then in the end the final product is showing you a dance that was created from the random move you made.    

Coach’s Eye - With this app you record yourself dancing. You go back and watch so you and your teacher can help give critiques. This app shows where and when you make a mistake or are a little off in your placement. You can draw on the screen and pause to show what needs to be fixed.

8counts - This app is very helpful to dancers who need to remember counts and what they need to do in a routine. This will give you a page to right correct counts and movements so you can check and see and be spot on.

Ballet Class iOS - To work on skill in dancing ballet is key. This app you can chose a piano song and lesson to go with your ballet warm up, and work. You can set your own speed and and bar levels to help adapt to new level and your routine.
