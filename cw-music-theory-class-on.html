3) Assigned - 3/23, Due - 4/14:  (No points, but needed for future 50pt test) Go to the Theory Graded Homework page and practice the listening to the ‘Popularly Used Diatonic Chord Progressions.’  First - You need to listen to bass note motion.  All of these chords are in Root Position and Diatonic though not all follow Classic Progression.  Second - Listen for the Major or Minor quality of the chords. Think logically.  Be prepared to have a listening test where you will identify these progressions. 

4) Assigned - 3/25, Due - 4/3:  (60 pts) Create a harmony for the included melody.  Remember that Classic Progression is always a good option, though not always required.  Plan on one or two chords per measure, though you can add a third chord if really necessary.  There must be at least 3 secondary chords (V7/X,  viio7/x or viio7(m7b5)/X) in your harmonic plan.  Don’t forget to give the chord name along with the Roman Numeral.

7)  Assigned - 4/14, Due 4/17:  (50pts)  In Assignemt 3, I asked you to listen to the most Popularly Used Chord Progressions.  Being able to indentify these will help your ear training and improve  your skill to reproduce your favorite songs with out having the music infront of you.  It’s time to take the listening Test on these progression.  Click here. 

8)  Asigned - 4/14, Due 4/22:  (50pts)  Complete the linked Harmonic Analysis.  Click Here.

9)   Assigned - 4/19, Due 4/29:  (No points, but needed for future 50pt test) Go to the Theory Graded Homework page and practice the listening to the ‘Popularly Used Diatonic Chord Progressions with Secondary Tonics.’  You will only be tested over the viio7/X and the V7/X progressions.  Each progression is 8 measures long and is repeated 8 times.  The first 4 measures are I-IV-V-I in C major to set up the coming transition.  The next two measures are filler chords of the key to lead to the Secondary V7 or viio7.  The last two measures contain the Secondary V7 or viio7 and then the destination chord.  First - You need to listen to bass note motion.  All of these chords are in Root Position.  So, the viio7/X resolves up a half step while the V7/x has a leap to resolve. Second - Listen for the Major or Minor quality of the destination chordand it’s relationship to the next measure that is a I. Think logically.  Be prepared to have a listening test where you will identify these progressions. 

10)  Assigned - 4/26, Due 5/1:  (50pts)  As you’ve learned by now, extended chords can get rather complicated.  Look at the two different attached Lead Sheets.  A Lead Sheet is a meoldy with the accompnying chords listed, but not voiced out.  Using the formulas dicussed in Assignment #5, spell each chord on the two pages. List their names and spellings on a seperate typed document.  Be aware that not all publishers use the same notation.  The -7 is a minor 7 chord, as in F-7 is the same as Fm7.  C+7 is a C augmented chord with a minor 7, it’s the same as C7#5.  Ignore the odd symbol at the end of the second line of All the Things You Are.  It means to repeat the previous chord.  You will also need these songs for the next assignment, #11.  Autumn Leaves    All The Things You Are

11)  Assigned - 4/26, Due 5/5:   (20pts)  Many traditional Jazz songs are built on what are called ii-V-I progressions.  That is, the root of one chord is a fifth away from the next and the next chord is a fifth away form the second chord.  Which is a second away from the first chord.  ie. A-D-G. Te way Classic Progression works.  Now, it doesn’t matter what the key signature says, what accidentals are added or what the quality of the chords are.  These chords may not be the real ii or V or I of the key.  They don’t even have to be chords of the same key.  We’re only looking at chord root relationships.  They can Pesudo ii-V-I’s.  The roots are not perfect 5th's apart, but the raw letter names are a 5th apart.  To make things even more fun, many times these chord progressions overlap.  The second chord of one ii-V-I is the first chord of the next ii-V-I and so on.  Using the two Lead Sheets from Assignment #10, I have marked the first couple systems of both with blue lines the represent the ii-V-I retionaships.  Continue what I started by marking any three chords in a row theat have a ii-V-I relationship.  Email me a copy of your completd page.

12)  Assigned - 4/26, Due 5/15:   (80pts)  Extended chord structures have been used extensively since the late 1800’s.  You’ll find them in Classical works just as often as Jazz.  It’s now time to start using them in your own compositions.  I have updated and given more advice on the project since it was posted.  Please check here to see the requirements for your composition.

Optional Extra Credit Assignments

1) (50pts) Find a poem you like and set it to an original melody with harmony.  If it’s a long poem, you’ll need to edit it.  This song can be written for vocal solo with piano or guitar or some type of vocal ensemble.  If you don’t want to do a vocal piece, do an instrumental.  An A-A-B-A structure is always a good go to option.  Try a piano piece solo if that works for you.  Send me a completed score.  

2)  (75pts)  Send me a recorded live performance of your composition.  This can be done with friends that are not all in the same location.  Ask me and I’ll coach you on how to do this.

3)  (25pts) My wife and daughter have Synesthesia, do you?  Huh?  What’s Synesthesia?  Is it dangerous?  No, Synesthsia is neurological phenomenon where your body gets one kind of sensory input (touch, sound, taste, sight or smell) and it triggers an involuntary response from one of your other senses.  You hear something but it keys a visual response. Studies vary, but about 10% of the population has sgnificant Synesthisic responses. Nearly everyone has some mild Synesthsia.  Have you ever heard a sound and your skin crawled?  Have you looked at a picure of your favorite food and without trying to, you can taste it?  To find out more, watch this short video.  You’ll find a lot of information on the web about Synesthesia.  You’ll even find quality on-line tests that will help you see if you have some Synesthisic traits.  Here’s one.  ( You will need to create a log-in.) If you take the test, send me a copy of your results and I’ll add 25 points to your extra credit grade.
