Music Theory, 1st Year and 2nd Year

1) Assigned - 3/23, Due - 3/27:  Go to MusicTheory.net and do the lesson on Diatonic Seventh Chords.  This website uses M7 for Major Seven.  I use mj7. Band In A Box uses maj7.  To review my notes on these chords and their fomulas, check here.

2) Assigned - 3/23, Due - 4/6:  Go to the Theory Graded Homework page and practice the listening to the ‘Popularly Used Diatonic Chord Progressions.’  Be prepared to have a listening test where you will identify these progressions when we return or at an earlier date if the 3 week period is extended. 

3) Assigned - 3/25, Due - 3/30: Take the test on Extended 7th Chords, Root Position

Women’s Chorale and Symphonic Choir

1) Assigned - 3/23, Due - 3/27:  Record yourself singing the text on your part along with the sound file for your choir.  Be sure to practice with the file multiple times before your record.  When you record, play the rehearsal track through some type of earphone and sing directly into your computer/phone/tablet.  While you should hear the rehearsal track, it should not be heard on your recording.  I only want your voice.  All of the parts are being played along with you to help you keep track of where you are in the song.  Each file begins with the ‘Do’ note.   Chorale - Sanctus.  You may want to review your part rehearsal file before you record the assignment.  Symphonic Choir - Ye Banks and Braes.  If the 3 week period is extended, you’ll be instructed to do this with other music in your folder.

2) Assigned  - 3/25, Due - 3/30:  Go to MusicTheory.net and take the test on identifing the four types of scales.  BE AWARE that the site does NOT call these scales by their Solfege names as we do.  You need to use the following terms:  Our regular D-R-M-F-S-L-T-D scale is ‘Major.'  The regular Minor scale is D-R-Meh-F-S-Leh-Teh-D is ‘Natural Minor.’  Altered minor, D-R-Meh-F-S-Leh-T-D is called 'Harmonic Minor.’  The odd minor scale, D-R-Meh-F-S-L-T-D-Teh-Leh-S-F-Meh-R-D is 'Melodic Minor.’  You need to earn at least 80% (32 out of 40).  Take the assignment as many times as you need to earn the score.  The site will play the scale from Do to Do ascending and then descending.

Performing Arts Class

1) Assigned - 3/23, Due - 3/27:  Due to the nature of our Silent Movie time-frame, the better movies rely on the use of Stock Characters.  Follow these links and research the nature of Stock Characters and their importance to story telling:  Youtube, Project Gutenberg.  Next you need to Download this page to create a list of a dozen types of your favorite kinds of Stock Characters, why you like them and what makes them unique.  

2) Assigned - 3/25, Due 3/30:  Silent Movies are based on scenarios, descriptions of actions, locations and events; not dialogue.  Create a Silent Movie scenario for 4 to 6 Stock Characters with 9 to 12 scenes.  Describe the actions, locations and events in each scene.  Be sure to use a traditional story arch with a beginning, rising action, climax and conclusion. Remember not every character needs to be in each scene.  Use this form.

Advanced Guitar 

1) Assigned - 3/23, Due - 3/27:  Record yourself playing the entire melody twice through for Blackberry Blossom along with the backing track sound file.  Before you do so, practice along with me as I play the assignment with the backing track to get a feeling of how it's done.

2) Assigned  - 3/25, Due 4/6:  Practice the following scale, 5th String Root Movable Major Scale.  Be prepared to perform it and identify each root note from 1st position (C#) to 7th position (G) and back to 1st position (Db).  That means you’ll say: C#-D-D#-E-F-F#-G-Gb-F-E-Eb-D-Db as you identify the roots in order.  Realize that you will identify the note under your fourth finger on the 5th string, but start and end playing the scale on the first finger 6th string.  This can be a bit confusing for some students.  This assignment is the 5th string version of the 6th String movable major scale that you were tested on a couple weeks ago. This will be the first skill test when we return or earlier if the 3 week period is extended.
