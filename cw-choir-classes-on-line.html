2) Assigned  - 3/25, Due - 3/30:  (40pts)  Go to MusicTheory.net and take the test on identifing the four types of scales.  BE AWARE that the site does NOT call these scales by their Solfege names as we do.  You need to use the following terms:  Our regular D-R-M-F-S-L-T-D scale is ‘Major.'  The regular Minor scale is D-R-Meh-F-S-Leh-Teh-D is ‘Natural Minor.’  Altered minor, D-R-Meh-F-S-Leh-T-D is called 'Harmonic Minor.’  The odd minor scale, D-R-Meh-F-S-L-T-D-Teh-Leh-S-F-Meh-R-D is 'Melodic Minor.’  You need to earn at least 80% (32 out of 40).  Take the assignment as many times as you need to earn the score.  The site will play the scale from Do to Do ascending and then descending.

Optional Extra Credit Assignments

1)  (20pts per lesson)   Since we can not be together in the CWHS Classroom for Guitar, you can take an On-Line Private lesson.  The lesson will be hosted through your Google Apps and will last about 20 minutes.  You can take a lesson as many times as you wish during our School closure.  You’ll earn Extra Credit points for each lesson up to the maximiun allowed, 60pts. We can work on general Guitar technique and/or any song or skill you wish.  To schedule your lesson, just email me at: tphillips@cwls.us.  We can use either Google Hangouts or Google Meet.  You should find these in your Google Dive Apps.  In Google Hangouts: It’s important that yo’ve set you go to the Custom Invite Setting and clicked Recmmended.  This allows me to call you directly through your school address.  In Google Meet:  I will email you a link to join the session.  

2)  (25pts) My wife and daughter have Synesthesia, do you?  Huh?  What’s Synesthesia?  Is it dangerous?  No, Synesthsia is neurological phenomenon where your body gets one kind of sensory input (touch, sound, taste, sight or smell) and it triggers an involuntary response from one of your other senses.  You hear something but it keys a visual response. Studies vary, but about 10% of the population has sgnificant Synesthisic responses. Nearly everyone has some mild Synesthsia.  Have you ever heard a sound and your skin crawled?  Have you looked at a picure of your favorite food and without trying to, you can taste it?  To find out more, watch this short video.  You’ll find a lot of information on the web about Synesthesia.  You’ll even find quality on-line tests that will help you see if you have some Synesthisic traits.  Here’s one.  ( You will need to create a log-in.) If you take the test, send me a copy of your results and I’ll add 25 points to your extra credit grade.

3)  (10 to 25pts)  Work on your Overtone Singing!  I talk a lot about learning how to tune your sound to produce Overtones, those high ringing sounds.  Here’s an assignment to help you improve your pitch and create overtones.  You’ll be using the Solfege lines we sing in class every morning.  Start by using some of the easier ones then progress to the more challenging ones. Even try them on a ‘Oh’ vowel.  The assignment is similar to what we’ve done in class.  You sing up and down your Solfege as a refence pitch is being played (sung).  The reference pitch is Letter name C, the same one we use as Do in warm-ups: Click Here.   1) Sing as slowly as you can manage.   2) Listen to how your voice locks with the reference pitch.  Can you create an Overtone?  Hold it steady and don’t waver in your vowel or breath.  3) Review your performance.  Did it lock imedidately or did you have to adjust the vowel or pitch a bit to get it set?  Try it again.  4)  You can do this exercise many different ways.  Use the Major Solfege, the Minor Solfege, call the reference pitch some other note, not Do. Call it So.  Call it Mi, ….  This will mean starting on a different Do, but it will help develop your skill to sing in tune in any key.  5)  Report back to me on how it went and record yourself singing one of our Solfege lines with the pitch playing.  The more dificult ones you achieve, the more points I’ll give you.
