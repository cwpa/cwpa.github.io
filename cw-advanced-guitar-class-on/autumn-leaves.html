Autumn Leaves

Autumn Leaves is found on page 107 in your book.  If you can’t find it, download it here.  You already have been working with nearly all of the chords to this song, but you’ll need to get two more under your fingers.  You’ll find video of how to play all the chords here.  Ignore the chords that I have marked out and notice that I have written in a Gm6 in numerous places.

All of the chords in this song get 4 strums except for the last 2 measures of the 7th line of music and right in the middle of the bottom line.  Those chords that split the measure only get 2 strums each.  Remember it’s better to cheat one chord to get to the next in time than to lose time because you’re slow getting to the next chord.

Here’s a graphic of the eight chord shapes you’ll need to play Autumn Leaves.  These are all movable shapes, so be sure to place them in the proper 

Take the time to see that you’re using the fingering I’ve listed.  It really is easier in the long run.

As you look down the page, mark which chords are 5th String and which are 6th String.  They pretty much alternate between 6th and 5th all the way through.  These are all movable shapes, so be sure to place them in the proper fret.  You’ll place the open circle in the chord chart above where you find the chord root letter name on that string.  

6th String: Cm7, Bbmaj7, Am7b5, Gm7, Gm6, Fm7

5th String: F9, Ebmaj7, D7, C9, Bb9

So, the first chord of the song is a 6th string root, Cm7.  C is found on the 8th fret of the 6th string.   The second chord is a 5th string root, F9.  F is also in the 8th fret, but on the 5th string. 

Go back to the first skill test of this semester where we located each letter name on the 4th, 5th and 6th strings and you’ll find all the correct locations.  You’ll find video of how and where to play all the chords here.

For your skill test you will need to play the song through twice.  Be sure to use the (G7) turn around at the bottom of the page before you go back to the start.  

You will also need to play the song with one of three accompaniment files.  You can choose slow (80) medium (90) or fast (100).  You can find the accompaniment files on this page.  you will want to rehearse the song with me before you record.  Here’s a play-along file.
