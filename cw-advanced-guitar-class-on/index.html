4th Quarter Grades - If you are not pleased with your grade, work on it and resubmit the assignment.  The goal is Mastery, not One and Done.

2) Assigned  - 3/25, Due 4/14:  (30pt test)  Practice the following scale, 5th String Root Movable Major Scale.  Be prepared to perform it and identify each root note from 1st position (C#) to 7th position (G) and back to 1st position (Db).  That means you’ll say: C#-D-D#-E-F-F#-G-Gb-F-E-Eb-D-Db as you identify the roots in order.  Realize that you will identify the note under your fourth finger on the 5th string, but start and end playing the scale on the first finger 6th string.  This can be a bit confusing for some students.  Watch this clip as I perform a portion of the skill to help clarify the process.  This assignment is the 5th string version of the 6th String movable major scale that you were tested on a couple weeks ago. 

3) Assigned - 3/25, Due  4/6:  (30pts)   Follow this link to an explanation of most used extended chord types and the most common fingerings.  Perform the included skill test as a video file.  

4) Assigned - 4/7, Due 4/24:  (30pts, 15pts per song)  The next step in finger picking is to your thumb independence and re-enforce your picking hand position.  Fingers 1,2 and 3 must be curved.  The Thumb is straight.  You are required to play two short Fingering Picking songs for this assignemnt.  Be sure to download the music and practice each one with the video file.

5)  Assigned - 4/26, Due 4/30:  (40pts)  Playing songs by ear is a valuable skill for any guitarist. The first step is to learn how to hear the individual chords of the moment and know how they fit into the 3 basic qualities of chords: Major Minor or 7.  We’ll start with Major vs. Minor.  I like to define Major as a brighter sound while Minor has a darker sound.  You might have your own way and that’s fine.  Follow this link to test your skill at identifying Major vs. Minor chords.  The program will play the 3 notes of the chord individually and then the notes together.  Listen for the quality of them combined.

2)  (25pts) My wife and daughter have Synesthesia, do you?  Huh?  What’s Synesthesia?  Is it dangerous?  No, Synesthsia is neurological phenomenon where your body gets one kind of sensory input (touch, sound, taste, sight or smell) and it triggers an involuntary response from one of your other senses.  You hear something but it keys a visual response. Studies vary, but about 10% of the population has sgnificant Synesthisic responses. Nearly everyone has some mild Synesthsia.  Have you ever heard a sound and your skin crawled?  Have you looked at a picure of your favorite food and without trying to, you can taste it?  To find out more, watch this short video.  You’ll find a lot of information on the web about Synesthesia.  You’ll even find quality on-line tests that will help you see if you have some Synesthisic traits.  Here’s one.  ( You will need to create a log-in.) If you take the test, send me a copy of your results and I’ll add 25 points to your extra credit grade.
